Figure 4-4. A typical nucleopetal melanocyte from the harderian gland of a leaden ( a/a;b/b;ln/ln) mouse. Note the two thin, sparsely pigmented dendrites and the aggregation of most of the pigment in the cell in an area near the nucleus. Approx., x1,100. (b) A typical nucleofugal melanocyte from the harderian gland of a nonagouti ( a/a) mouse. Note the dispersion of a large fraction of the melanin granules of the cell into the five large dendrites. Approx., x450. From C.L. Markert and W.K. Silvers, 1959. Reproduced with permission of Academic Press Inc.
