Figure 4-6. Pigment granules in awl hairs of six different genotypes. The large masses are medullary cells while the streaks of granules are from the overlying cortex. In this drawing, the yellow phaeomelanin granules of the Ay mouse are symbolized by open circles; no distinction is made pretween black and brown eumelanin. In the medullary cells of yellow mice the nucleus is usually visible. Note that whereas the granules in intensely pigmented black ( a/a;B/B) hairs are usually either long-oval or oval, those in brown ( a/a;b/b) hairs are round. Note also that the substitution of cch/cch for C/C (on an a/a;B/B background) results in a diminution of granule size and not number. Finally note the clumping of granules in the d/d hair and the shred-shaped granules in the p/p shaft. These drawings were made by Dr. E.S. Russell and appeared in H. Grüneberg ( 1952).
