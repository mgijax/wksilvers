Figure 3-13. Hair bulbs that developed in (a) a graft of intact somites plus neural tube taken from 10.5-day-old albino ( c/c) embryo. This graft possessed neural crest cells, and clear cells (arrows) are present. (b) A graft from the limb bud of a 10 to 10.5-day-old albino ( c/c) embryo. This graft did not include neural crest cells, and note the absence of clear cells and the similarity to bulbs shown in Figs. 3-10 and 3-11. (c) A neural crest-free limb bud graft from a 10.5-day-old potentially pigmented ( a/a;b/b;d/d) embryo. Note similarity to (b). (d) A graft from the limb bud of a 12.5-day-old albino ( c/c) embryo. This graft included neural crest, and as in (a) clear cells are present (arrows). Approx., x600. From W.K. Silvers, 1958c. Reproduced with permission of The Wistar Institute Press.
